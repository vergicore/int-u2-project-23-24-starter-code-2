Gun Violence


"Gun violence is a leading cause of premature death in the U.S. Guns kill more than 38,000 people and cause nearly 85,000 injuries each year. As a longtime advocate for violence prevention policies, APHA recognizes a comprehensive public health approach to addressing this growing crisis is necessary." -apha.org

Overview

Gun Violence is an enduring issue that has gone on for centuries. Every day, more than 600 people die due to incidents involving firearms. This is partially caused by how easy it is to actually acquire a fire arm, whether through legal, or illegal means. The people affected by this problem the most have been found to usually be people of color, deprived communities, or other marginalized groups. These incidents are harmful because they can disrupt our access to healthcare and education as people may be too scared to go out and attend these facilities because of the presence of firearms in their communities

Cited from Amnesty.org

How can we help?

Some solutions that could help to prevent gun violence are...

  1. Stricter firearm laws: The laws will make it harder for people to have acess to dangerous weapons.
  2. Establishing a culture of gun safety: Requiring safe and secure storage of firearms, mandatory firearm training, required licences, and reducing access of firearms to the youth and young individuals who could potentially harm themselves are all good first steps to preventing gun violence and creating a safer environment.
  3. Advance gun safety and self-defense technology: Innovating new technology that can help people defend themselves and enhance gun safety are all good ways to prevent and stop gun violence.
Cited from PreventionInstitute.org

Statistics

This year, 117,345 people in the U.S were shot. Among those:

  • 42,654 people die from gun violence
  • 16,651 are murdered
  • 76,725 people survive gunshot injuries
  • 34,566 are intentionally shot by someone else and survive
  • 24,569 die from gun suicide
  • 3,554 survive an attempted gun suicide
  • 503 killed unintentionally
  • 552 are killed by legal intervention
  • 1,376 are shot by legal intervention and survive
  • 379 die but the intent was unknown
  • 4,471 are shot and survive but the intent is unknown
  • 547 women are killed by their husband or male dating partner**
